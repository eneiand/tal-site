Natalya AndersonWriter

Natalya Anderson has been a writer, editor and often-injured ballet dancer for over 16 years. She completed a Masters in Creative Writing at Anglia Ruskin University in Cambridge, England, and a Bachelor of Journalism at Ryerson University in Toronto, Canada.

When she is not monologuing about the torments of her latest poem or short story, Natalya gives her heart and soul to her husband, their son and their son's Future Siblings.

Poems

  • Bath Bubble

    How do you go about calming

    the sting dangerously offshore

    of her bikini line, from over-

    shaving, over-exfoliating over

    the scars that thread through

    dreams which she wears

    thin, lest they absolve her spirit

    for wanting cheese, a chocolate

    bar, spaghetti, a slap across her mouth

  • Stockholm

    Under hot weight, frozen until snores signalled

    the all clear. She wriggled down sheets, hair

    the last piece trapped before she crept and traced

    cold, paisley walls whose frayed seams blemished

    fingertips. She paused, dug onward until whiffing

    sleep breath. Conjuring a genesis of light, her hand

    found other fingers. Gripping, the two stumbled past

    the Norman Rockwell into the sundeck where they cradled

    arms, legs, tightly woven, waited for the hawks to cry out

  • The Woman In Clericals

    In the hollow of my mother’s bed,

    on flannel sheets she sprinkled with freesia-

    scented talc, she rolls onto her side, leaves

    an impression of warmth. I burrow

    into her dented cocoon, reach out for her

    fleeting body, listening for the rasp

    of St Francis of Assisi carved with heavenly

    creatures, dragging along her thick silver chain.

    It stops, wedges between her breasts

    to announce that she is rising. I follow,

    disciplined by the sudden cold; fold

    the sheets back over her creases. All day

    I wait, thinking about her warm hands:

    palms underneath my toes, thumbs and

    fingers wrapped around to meet each other.
